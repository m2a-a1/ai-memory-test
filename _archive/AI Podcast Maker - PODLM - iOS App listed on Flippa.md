**Operations**

PODLM is an AI-powered mobile application that transforms text, notes, and PDF documents into professional podcast-quality audio content. The app combines large language model (LLM) technology for script generation with text-to-speech (TTS) for multi-voice narration — all within a single, intuitive workflow.

Revenue is generated through two channels: recurring subscriptions for power users and one-time credit purchases for casual users, both managed through Adapty. This dual model captures a wider audience and provides both predictable recurring income and transactional upside.

The tech stack includes Flutter for the frontend, Firebase for backend/authentication/storage, Google Gemini and OpenAI ChatGPT APIs for AI script generation, and Google Cloud Voices for high-quality text-to-speech output.

To keep the app operational, the new owner would need to maintain API keys for Gemini, ChatGPT, and Google Cloud TTS, along with the Firebase project and Adapty subscription management. Estimated weekly time commitment is 3-5 hours, primarily for monitoring API usage/costs, responding to user reviews, and occasional updates. No dedicated server infrastructure is required — the app runs entirely on serverless architecture (Firebase), keeping operational costs low and scalable.

**Users**

PODLM has accumulated 28,800+ downloads with 3.36 average sessions per active device, indicating solid user engagement and repeat usage. The app attracts content creators, educators, students, business professionals, and anyone looking to convert written content into audio format.

User acquisition is predominantly organic. App Store Search accounts for 37.7% of all downloads (10,838), while App Referrer drives 40.3% (11,579). Web Referrer contributes 15% (4,298) and App Store Browse adds 6.3% (1,818). This organic-heavy acquisition profile means the app has strong App Store Optimization (ASO) and does not depend on paid advertising to maintain download volume.

Geographically, the user base is concentrated in high-value markets: United States leads with 8,114 downloads, followed by Taiwan (4,274), Germany (1,669), Hong Kong (1,388), and the United Kingdom (967). The US-dominant profile is particularly attractive for monetization as these users have the highest willingness to pay for subscription products.

Product page views have reached 53,500+ with a 7.31% conversion rate, and total App Store impressions stand at 558,000+ — demonstrating significant brand visibility in the AI podcast niche.

**Financials**

Annual revenue over the past 12 months is approximately $4,600, with lifetime proceeds of $5,470. Current monthly revenue runs at approximately $300-600/month with a stable upward trajectory.

Proceeds per paying user average $8.73 weekly, reflecting healthy per-user monetization. The dual revenue model (subscriptions + credits) provides flexibility — subscribers generate predictable recurring revenue while credit purchases add incremental income from casual users.

Operational costs are minimal due to the serverless Firebase architecture. Primary costs include API usage fees (Gemini, ChatGPT, Google Cloud TTS), Adapty subscription management, and the Apple Developer account. Total monthly operating costs are estimated at $10-15, resulting in healthy profit margins of approximately 70-80%.

There is no significant seasonality in revenue. Growth has been steady since the app's launch in October 2024, with the largest opportunity for revenue acceleration being Android expansion, price optimization, and targeted marketing to the education and content creator segments.

**Additional Notes**

PODLM was launched in October 2024 and has grown entirely through organic channels. The app was built from scratch (not a reskin) using Flutter, making it ready for Android deployment with minimal additional development — this represents the single largest untapped growth opportunity.

The sale includes full source code, Firebase project, Adapty account, App Store listing, all creative assets, and documentation. The new owner will have everything needed for a seamless transition.

Key growth opportunities for the new owner include: launching on Google Play Store to capture the Android market, implementing a freemium web version for broader reach, pursuing enterprise and education licensing deals, adding features like podcast hosting/distribution integration, and exploring partnerships with content platforms. The AI podcast creation market is in its early stages and growing rapidly, with Google's NotebookLM validating the concept at scale — PODLM is well-positioned as a mobile-native alternative in this expanding space.
