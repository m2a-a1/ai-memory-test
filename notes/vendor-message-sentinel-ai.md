## Message

**Subject:** Serious Inquiry — Sentinel AI / Due Diligence Questions Before Moving Forward

---

Hi,

I came across your Sentinel AI listing on Flippa and I've been reviewing it carefully. The product addresses a space I know well — enterprise AI governance, LLMOps tooling, and security infrastructure — and I operate in a B2B Cloud/DevOps environment where this kind of control plane could be a meaningful fit, either as an internal tool or as part of a broader product offering.

I've completed an initial review and I'm genuinely interested in moving forward. Before I can make a serious offer, I need to work through a structured due diligence process. The questions below are the ones I need answered — some are straightforward, a few will require you to share access or documentation.

I've organized them by topic to keep this efficient.

---

### 1. Codebase & Technical Quality

1. Are you willing to provide read-only access to the full codebase (private GitHub repo or similar) under NDA? I want to perform a technical review before any offer.
2. What is the approximate line count / number of modules? Is there an architectural overview or README that covers the full system design?
3. What is the current state of test coverage? Are there automated tests (unit, integration, e2e)?
4. Are there any known bugs, incomplete features, or items left on the roadmap that were not shipped?
5. Are there any third-party libraries or components with restrictive licenses (GPL, AGPL, SSPL) embedded in the product that would constrain commercial use or redistribution?

---

### 2. Infrastructure & Operational Costs

6. Where is the product currently hosted and what does the monthly infrastructure cost? (Vercel, Railway, AWS, self-hosted, etc.)
7. Are there any external services with paid plans currently active (e.g., ClickHouse managed, Redis cloud, auth providers, email delivery)? What is the monthly spend on these?
8. Can the full infrastructure be migrated or redeployed independently, or are there dependencies on accounts that cannot be transferred?

---

### 3. Users & Traction

9. The listing mentions 10 active subscribers — are these paying customers, beta users, or demo/test accounts? If any are paying, what is the pricing and total MRR?
10. How did these users find the product? Were they personally recruited, inbound from a launch post, or existing contacts?
11. Have any of them churned or expressed intent to churn?

---

### 4. Backlink Profile & Domain

12. Ahrefs shows ~66 referring domains on sentinel-ai.dev as of May 2026, growing from 0 in 3 months. Can you give me a breakdown of the main sources — was this driven by a ProductHunt launch, HackerNews post, GitHub activity, or something else?
13. Do you own the domain outright, and is it included in the sale?

---

### 5. IP Ownership & Legal

14. Was the entire codebase written by you personally, or did you use contractors or AI-assisted generation tools? Is there any IP ambiguity regarding authorship or ownership?
15. Are there any existing agreements with users (e.g., free trial terms, commitments around uptime, support) that would transfer to the buyer?
16. Is there any pending dispute, DMCA claim, or legal matter related to the product or domain?

---

### 6. Motivation & Transition

17. You built a substantial product in 3 months and are selling before any monetization attempt — what's the honest reason for the sale? Is it purely commercial fit ("best suited for the right acquirer"), or did you run into specific obstacles (market access, fundraising, competing priorities)?
18. What level of post-sale support are you willing to provide, and for how long? (Handover calls, documentation, bug fixes during transition)
19. Is this listing exclusive to Flippa, or are you in discussions with other potential buyers?

---

I realize this is a longer list than you might typically receive. I'm asking because I take this seriously — and if the answers check out, I can move quickly. I'm not looking to negotiate you down arbitrarily; I'm looking to understand what I'm actually buying.

Happy to jump on a call if that's easier for some of these.

Best,
Marc-Antoine
