## Listing Description

**StayLayer** is a completed pre-launch hospitality SaaS built for independent villas, boutique hotels, B&Bs, guest houses, and glamping brands that want a direct, branded online presence without relying entirely on marketplaces or generic site builders.

This is not a content template, a mock landing page, or a half-finished idea. It is a structured multi-tenant software platform with a clear niche and a clear commercial angle: helping hospitality operators launch and manage inquiry-first websites with publishing, SEO, multilingual content, domains, billing, and operations in one product.

**What is included in the product?**

StayLayer already includes the full product shape:

\- public marketing funnel  
\- customer dashboard  
\- live tenant-aware website runtime  
\- internal operator console  
\- shared component system and editor foundation

Inside the customer workspace, the product already supports:

\- visual page editing  
\- draft and preview workflow  
\- publishing and deployment flow  
\- custom domains  
\- inquiry forms and inbox workflows  
\- SEO operations  
\- multilingual publishing and translation workflows  
\- billing and usage surfaces  
\- support workflow  
\- workspace and site administration

Inside the operator console, the buyer gets internal business tooling for:

\- tenant oversight  
\- support operations  
\- billing operations  
\- domains and deployments  
\- SEO and translation operations  
\- permissions and operator account controls  
\- broader platform administration

**Why this matters?**

The strongest value of StayLayer is not that it exists in a market without competition. The hospitality software market already has large players. The opportunity here is that most of those players are either too broad, too legacy, too marketplace-driven, too booking-engine-centric, or too fragmented across multiple tools.

StayLayer is attractive because it takes a narrower and more practical position:

\- inquiry-first instead of pretending to be an all-in-one PMS  
\- hospitality-focused instead of generic website-builder software  
\- structured as a real SaaS platform instead of a theme or agency starter pack  
\- built around direct demand, SEO, multilingual publishing, and operational control

That means a buyer is not trying to outspend giant incumbents head-on. The more realistic path is to own a tighter niche and offer a cleaner product for operators who want branded direct-demand infrastructure without enterprise complexity.

**Why a buyer could win with this?**

A buyer does not need to believe that StayLayer will instantly beat every large competitor. The real case is that the product is already far enough along to give the next owner a faster route to market than building from scratch.

**The buyer gets:**

\- a clear niche  
\- a complete pre-launch product foundation  
\- customer-side and operator-side tooling  
\- strong positioning around hospitality direct demand  
\- multiple monetizable surfaces already built into the platform

The next owner can win by executing better in a focused lane:

\- target independent hospitality brands underserved by larger platforms  
\- position around direct inquiry and brand control  
\- sell multilingual publishing and SEO as premium value  
\- partner with hospitality-focused agencies or consultants  
\- turn the existing product depth into a sharper go-to-market strategy

**Current business status**

StayLayer is a pre-launch asset. It has **not** been publicly launched, so there is **no** revenue history, no customer base, and no meaningful traffic to report yet.

That should be understood as part of the opportunity, not hidden as a weakness. This is **not** an MRR acquisition. It is a **product-and-positioning acquisition** for a buyer who wants a serious software foundation without spending the time and money to build the entire platform from zero.

**Why I am selling?**

I brought StayLayer to the point where the product architecture, niche, and software shape are all clear. The next stage is commercialization, partnerships, launch strategy, and focused buyer execution. Rather than taking it through that phase myself, I am looking for the right buyer to take over and push it into market.

**What is included in the sale**

**\- full source code**  
**\- marketing app**  
**\- customer dashboard**  
**\- website runtime**  
**\- operator console**  
**\- shared component and editor foundation**  
**\- technical docs and implementation planning material**  
**\- existing product positioning work**
